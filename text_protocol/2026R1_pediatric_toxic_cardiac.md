
# Toxic Ingestion / Exposure / Overdose – Cardiotoxic Medications (Pediatric Protocol)

## Overall Procedure
1. UNIVERSAL PATIENT CARE PROTOCOL
2. DIAGNOSTIC EKG PROCEDURE and Assess Vitals
3. Treat Per Presenting EKG Rhythm
    1. Circulation / Shock Trauma Cardiac Medical Airway / Breathing
    2. Contact Medical Control for the use of vasopressors if failed fluid bolus
4. BLOOD GLUCOSE PROCEDURE
5. IV / IO PROCEDURE
6. CAPNOGRAPHY PROCEDURE
7. AIRWAY PROTOCOL / OXYGEN
8. Transport to appropriate facility, Contact receiving facility, Consult Medical Control where indicated, Appropriate transfer of care
9. Consider restraints if necessary for patient's and / or personnel's protection per the Restraint Procedure
10. If it can be done safely, take whatever container the substance came from to the hospital along with readily obtainable samples of medication unless this results in an unreasonable delay of transport
11. If applicable, DO NOT transport a patient to the hospital until properly decontaminated

### Bradycardia (Potential Causes: Calcium Channel Blockers, Beta Blockers "LOL" Drugs)
1. CALCIUM CHLORIDE 20 mg/kg IV/IO (Max 1 gram) **or** CALCIUM GLUCONATE 60 mg/kg IV/IO (Max 3 grams) over 10 min
    1. May repeat either if available and EKG changes reoccur
2. May CONSIDER ATROPINE 0.02 mg/kg IV/IO, repeat every 3–5 minutes. 
    * Min dose 0.1 mg
    * Max dose 0.5 mg child
    * Max dose 1 mg Adolescent

### QRS Prolongation/Widening (Potential Causes: Sodium Channel Blocker / Tricyclic Anti-Depressants)
1. SODIUM BICARBONATE 1 mEq/kg SLOW, Max 50 mEq IV/IO, Until QRS narrows / condition improves
    1. If no improvement, Contact ONLINE MEDICAL CONTROL
    2. May repeat if available and EKG changes reoccur
2. Prophylactically apply pacing/defib pads and prepare for decompensation
   * AVR – R wave final part of QRS
   * QRS >120ms (3 small EKG boxes)

### Prolonged QT / Torsades (Potential Causes: Potassium Channel Blockers)
1. MAGNESIUM SULFATE 25 mg/kg IV/IO, Max 2000 mg (2 grams) in 100 ml D5 over 20 min
    * To reduce risk of Torsades
    * Does not address the Potassium blockade
    * Supportive Care
2. Witnessed episodes of Torsades or QT>500ms (2.5 large EKG boxes)

### Refractory Shock/Circulatory Compromise
1. IV NORMAL SALINE BOLUS 20 ml/kg to Maintain MAP > 65 or SBP 90 if MAP Unavailable or Radial Pulses
2. EPINEPHrine PUSH DOSE: Make 10 mcg/ml, 1 mcg/kg (Max dose 10 mcg) every 2–5 min – slow push, titrate to effect
3. TRANSCUTANEOUS PACING PROCEDURE

## Indications
* Ingestion or suspected ingestion of a potentially toxic substance

## Signs and Symptoms
* Mental status changes
* Hypo/hypertension
* Decreased respiratory rate
* Tachycardia, dysrhythmias
* Seizures

## Differential Diagnosis
* Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs)
* Acetaminophen (Tylenol)
* Depressants
* Stimulants
* Anticholinergic
* Cardiac medications
* Solvents, alcohols, cleaning agents
* Insecticides (organophosphates)
* Respiratory depression
* Other organophosphates
* Carbamates

## Key Points
* Exam: Mental Status, Skin, HEENT, Heart, Lungs, Abdomen, Extremities, Neuro
* Do not rely on patient history of ingestion, especially in suicide attempts.
* Bring bottles, contents, and emesis to ED.
* Tricyclics: 4 major areas of toxicity: seizures, dysrhythmias, hypotension, decreased mental status or coma; rapid progression from alert mental status to death.
* Acetaminophen: initially normal or nausea/vomiting. If not detected and treated, causes irreversible liver failure.
* Depressants: decreased HR, decreased BP, decreased temperature, decreased respirations, non-specific pupils.
* Stimulants: increased HR, increased BP, increased temperature, dilated pupils, and seizures.
* Anticholinergics: increased HR, increased temperature, dilated pupils, and mental status changes.
* Cardiac Medications: dysrhythmias and mental status changes.
* Solvents: nausea, vomiting, and mental status changes.
* Insecticides: increased or decreased HR, increased secretions, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, pinpoint pupils.
