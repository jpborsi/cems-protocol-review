
# Pediatric Protocol: Nausea / Vomiting >1 yr. Old

## Overall Procedure
1. UNIVERSAL PATIENT CARE PROTOCOL
2. Administer Oxygen
3. IV PROCEDURE
4. Patient has Nausea / Vomiting
5. Continue with further steps as necessary
    1. If EKG is obtained and QT > 500ms (2.5 large EKG boxes), do not administer Ondansetron.
    2. Transport to appropriate facility.
    3. Contact receiving facility.
    4. Consult Medical Control where indicated.
    5. Appropriate transfer of care.

### Ondansetron Administration
1. ONDANSETRON 0.15 mg/kg IM or IV over 2 - 4 minutes (for patients > 1 year)
    - OR 0.15 mg/kg Injectable Liquid PO > 1 year
    - Repeat every 10 min as needed; Max 4 mg
2. OR
    - ONDANSETRON Oral Dissolving Tabs 4 mg Oral > 40 kg AND 12 years
    - No repeat

## Indications
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Medication(s) administration such as narcotic analgesics

## Signs and Symptoms
* Complaints of nausea and/or vomiting
* Consider AMI / diagnostic EKG

## Differential Diagnosis
* Gastroenteritis
* Toxic ingestion / food poisoning
* Bowel obstruction
* Appendicitis
* Gastritis
* Cholecystitis (gallbladder)
* Hepatitis / cirrhosis
* Headaches / migraine
* Pregnancy
* Hypertensive crisis
* Electrolyte imbalances
* DKA
* Intracranial pressure
* Sepsis / infections

## Key Points
* Position patient to protect airway as appropriate. (Recovery position, sitting up, etc.)
* Immediately position entire patient or their head to side if patient begins vomiting then retrieve suction.
* Patients with altered LOC and nausea / vomiting need to have airway maintenance prioritized before medication.
* Prepare and test suction prior to its need.
* Give Ondansetron over at least 2 minutes, slow IV.
* Treat patients early, no need to wait for patient to begin vomiting to administer Ondansetron.
* Patients receiving medications such as narcotic analgesics may require concurrent administration of Ondansetron to reduce nausea associated with such medications.
